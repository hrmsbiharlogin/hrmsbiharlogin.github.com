HRMS Bihar GPF Statement Online 2026 – Check General Provident Fund Balance

Complete guide for Bihar government employees to check and download their GPF statement from the HRMS Bihar portal. Know your balance, interest earned, and withdrawal history.

What is GPF for Bihar Government Employees?

The General Provident Fund (GPF) is a mandatory savings scheme for Bihar state government employees who were recruited before January 1, 2004. Every month, a fixed percentage of your basic salary is automatically deducted and deposited into your GPF account. The Government of Bihar pays interest on this accumulated balance — and the entire amount, including interest, is paid out when you retire.

GPF is different from NPS (National Pension System), which covers employees recruited after 2004. If you are unsure which scheme you are under, check your HRMS Bihar salary slip — it clearly shows either GPF or NPS deduction every month.

According to Wikipedia's overview of General Provident Fund, GPF is one of India's oldest government savings instruments, providing financial security to civil servants at retirement. Bihar follows the Central Government GPF rules with state-specific modifications.

✅ Who Has GPF in Bihar Government?
Bihar state government employees recruited before 1 January 2004 — including teachers, police, revenue employees, health workers, and administrative staff — are covered under GPF. Employees recruited after that date are covered under NPS instead.

How to Check GPF Balance on HRMS Bihar Portal

The Bihar Finance Department has integrated GPF statements into the HRMS Bihar portal at hrms.bihar.gov.in, making it easy to check your GPF balance anytime without visiting any office. Here are the exact steps:

  1. Open Google Chrome and visit hrms.bihar.gov.in. Enter your Employee ID and password to login. Need help? See our guide to reset your HRMS Bihar password if you have forgotten it.
  2. After login, look at your dashboard menu. Find the Finance section or look for a menu labelled GPF, Provident Fund, or Employee Finance. Click on it.
  3. From the submenu, select 'GPF Statement' or 'GPF Account Details'.
  4. A filter will appear — select the Financial Year for which you want the statement. For example, 2025-26 for the current year.
  5. Click View or Submit. Your complete GPF statement will load showing: opening balance, monthly contributions, interest credited, withdrawals made, and closing balance. Press Ctrl+P → Save as PDF to download.

Understanding Your GPF Statement

Once your statement loads, here is what each section means:

Field on StatementWhat It Means
GPF Account NumberYour unique GPF account ID — quote this in all GPF-related applications
Opening BalanceGPF balance at the start of the financial year
Monthly SubscriptionAmount deducted from your salary each month as GPF contribution
Interest CreditedAnnual interest calculated at the declared rate (7.1% for 2025-26)
Withdrawals / AdvancesAny partial withdrawals or advances taken during the year
Closing BalanceYour total GPF balance at year end — this is your current savings

GPF Withdrawal Rules for Bihar Government Employees

Bihar government employees can make partial withdrawals from their GPF account for specific approved purposes. This is governed by the General Provident Fund (Central Services) Rules. Here are the allowed purposes:

  1. Medical Emergency — For treatment of self or dependent family member for serious illness. No repayment required after 10 years of service.
  2. Education — For higher education of your children, including professional courses. Allowed after 15 years of service.
  3. Marriage — For the marriage of your children or self (for female employees). Maximum 3 months basic pay or 50% of GPF balance, whichever is less.
  4. Housing — For construction, purchase, or major repair of a house. Submit application through your DDO with relevant documents.
  5. Final Withdrawal at Retirement — Complete GPF balance including all interest is paid out at retirement.
⚠️ How to Apply for GPF Withdrawal
GPF withdrawal cannot be applied directly through the HRMS Bihar portal yet. You must submit a written application to your DDO (Drawing and Disbursing Officer) along with supporting documents. Your DDO processes the application through the Bihar Finance Department. Keep a copy of your GPF statement from HRMS Bihar as reference.

GPF vs NPS — Which Do You Have?

Many Bihar government employees are confused about whether they have GPF or NPS. The easiest way to check is through your monthly salary slip available on HRMS Bihar. If the deduction column shows "GPF" — you are under the old pension scheme. If it shows "NPS" — you are under the National Pension System.

For a detailed comparison, the Wikipedia article on India's National Pension System explains the difference between the two schemes clearly. In general, GPF guarantees a fixed interest rate set by the government, while NPS returns depend on market performance.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often is interest credited to HRMS Bihar GPF account?+
GPF interest is calculated on the minimum balance between the 10th and last day of each month, and credited to the account annually at the end of the financial year (March 31). The rate for 2025-26 is 7.1% per annum, applicable to all Bihar state government GPF accounts.
My GPF statement is not showing on HRMS Bihar — what to do?+
If GPF statement is not visible, first confirm that you are covered under GPF (pre-2004 recruitment). If yes, clear browser cache and try again. Some districts may have pending data migration. Contact your District Treasury Office or call HRMS helpdesk at 0612-2217601.
Is the GPF amount shown on HRMS Bihar guaranteed?+
Yes. GPF is a government-guaranteed savings scheme. The balance shown on your HRMS Bihar GPF statement reflects the actual amount in your account as maintained by the Bihar Finance Department. Unlike NPS, there is no market risk with GPF.
